# Lesson 5: I'm getting

When combine the words "I am" and "getting", you are telling someone
"you" are gaining possession, being affected by or have plans to seek out
and obtain a particular thing.

Here are some examples:

- "I'm getting better."
- "I'm getting ready for bed."
- "I'm getting a tooth ache."
- "I'm getting a cold."
- "I'm getting married."
- "I'm getting tired."
- "I'm getting good at reading."
- "I'm getting a new car."
- "I'm getting a job."
- "I'm getting a puppy."
