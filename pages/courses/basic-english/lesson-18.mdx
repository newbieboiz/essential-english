# Lesson 18: I didn't mean to + (verb)

The word "didn't" is a contraction of the words "did not".
When using it in a sentence with the words "mean to" you are informing someone that
you did something you regret or are sorry for.
This could have been a physical, mental or verbal action.

Here are some examples:

- "I didn't mean to hurt your feeling."
- "I didn't mean to call you so late."
- "I didn't mean to lie about what happened."
- "I didn't mean to embarrass you."
- "I didn't mean to stay out so late."
- "I didn't mean to say those things."
- "I didn't mean to leave you out."
- "I didn't mean to make you confused."
- "I didn't mean to think you were involved."
- "I didn't mean to cause trouble."
